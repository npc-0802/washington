Washington: variable purchase price, variable down payment, a $250k cash pool allocated first to down payment and then to renovation, an independently adjustable after-renovation value, and optional purchased points financed through HELOC. Renovation cash shortfalls and points are modeled as HELOC debt; the potential refi is modeled but not assumed guaranteed. Each purchased point costs $9,500, is financed, and lowers the potential refi rate by 0.25 percentage points. Turnkey uses a 25% down payment plus the selected purchase price and a 5.875% mortgage. Renter invests the savings versus the cheaper ownership option at 10% annually. All estimates.
Simplification note: Washington non-mortgage carrying costs are held constant for slider changes, so higher projected finished values do not trigger higher modeled taxes or insurance.
